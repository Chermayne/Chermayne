In October 2019, I left my career of 5 years in market research operations and management to focus on a career in growing food.

I'm currently working freelance and volunteering on various vegetable farms, my focus being on growing food for local communities to create closer knit communities, giving people insight into how food is produced, and focusing on ensuring that small-scale farming can be a sustainable way of life. All major previous experiences are also mentioned below.

CoFarm Cambridge

March 2020 - present
Volunteer / freelance

I support the CoFarm Cambridge site by running volunteer and harvest days, alongside 3 other members of the core team. The market garden, in it's first season on less than 2 acres, has raised over 4 tonnes of food for local food hubs around Cambridge.

At my time here I've learnt alot about what it takes to start a market garden in an empty field. It's taught me about the power of local support as we could not have gone through the first season without a strong regular group of passionate volunteers, and I've also learnt a lot more about looking at the farm as an ecosystem, looking more in-depth into the role of flowers, trees, shrubes, and insects.

CoFarm Cambridge, sector: Agriculture & Horticulture

Repowering London - Energy Garden

November 2019 - April 2020
Energy Garden Project Support & Comms Officer (freelance)

Repowering London is a non-profit organisation that focuses on renewable community energy. I am currently working part time on their Energy Garden project that aims to utilise disused Overground spaces by turning them into community gardens, in efforts to tackle climate change, encourage biodiversity in London by creating green corridors, and integrate nature into the everyday lives of London commuters.

I am supporting on project management and operations by organising and managing plans/events for the gardens, managing community engagagement, funding research, systems management, and garden maintence.

I've also worked on a site redesign by focusing and advising on user-experience design as well as analysed their site and social media data to inform, build, and execute a social media comms strategy.

Repowering London, sector: Community Gardens & Community Energy
